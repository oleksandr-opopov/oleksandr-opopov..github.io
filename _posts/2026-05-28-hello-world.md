Every developer blog starts with a "Hello, World" post. This is mine.

## Why I'm Writing

I've been building software for over seven years. In that time, I've accumulated a lot of knowledge — patterns that work, pitfalls that don't, tools I wish I'd known about sooner. Most of that knowledge lives in Slack threads, Notion docs, and the back of my mind.

Writing is how I turn that into something shareable. If even one post saves another developer an hour of debugging or helps them see a problem differently, it was worth writing.

## What To Expect

I'll be writing about things I'm actively working with and thinking about:

- **Full-stack architecture** — design decisions, trade-offs, and lessons from production
- **Developer tools** — CLIs, build systems, and the tools that make coding better
- **Performance & scaling** — what I've learned making systems faster under load
- **Career & craft** — thoughts on growing as a software engineer

I'm not aiming for a content calendar or SEO-optimized posts. Just honest, useful writing when I have something worth saying.

## Technical Stack

This blog is built with [Jekyll](https://jekyllrb.com/) and hosted on GitHub Pages. Posts are written in Markdown, which means the barrier to publishing is low — open an editor, write, commit, push. Done.

If you want to follow along, there's an [RSS feed](/feed.xml) you can subscribe to.
